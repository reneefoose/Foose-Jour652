I teach graduate level courses to students seeking master’s and doctorate degrees in educational leadership. As an advisor, I mentor students through the dissertation process and assist them with research endeavors. I am also a Fellow in the Center for Excellence in Teaching and Learning where I work with junior and tenured faculty to design and deliver lessons to increase student engagement. My research focus is organizational culture and public-school reform.

Executive Level Administrative Experience

  • Superintendent, Howard County Public Schools –

    • Overall operation of the 95th largest school system in the United States to include 55,000 students, 8,000 staff members in 76 schools and offices with a 900 million operating budget and over 1 billion capital budgets. Provided organizational leadership to ensure the implementation of the Board of Education's goals and academic priorities and the school system's strategic plan. Howard County is the highest performing school system in Maryland and one of the highest performing in the region. Geographically located between Baltimore and Washington, D.C., Howard County’s student demographic is majority minority and outpaces the region and nation on achievement gap elimination.

  • Deputy Superintendent, Baltimore County Public Schools –

    • Direct oversight and responsibility for instructional and operational function and performance of the 24th largest school system in the United States that includes 173 schools, 104,000 students, 17,000 staff members and a 1.4-billion-dollar annual budget. Specific duties included acting for, represented, and exercised the authority of the superintendent in directing the implementation of school system plans, policies, and programs. Coordinated the activities of eight associate superintendents for the effective and efficient operation of all schools and offices. Responsible for the oversight and integrated management of the human resources, curriculum and instruction, and all support services-departments to include: facilities management; financial services; information technology, budget, and strategic planning; materials management; transportation; capital improvements and construction; food and nutrition services; maintenance; school plant operations; and functions of the school system.

  • Associate Superintendent, Montgomery County Public Schools –

    • Direct evaluative oversight for all accountability that serves the 16th largest school system in the United States that includes 200 schools, 143,000 students, 20,000 staff members and a 2.1-billion-dollar annual budget. Specifically, duties included acting for, representing, and exercising the authority of the superintendent and deputy superintendent in directing the implementation of the school system's strategic plans, initiatives, policies, and programs. Managed the activities of the reporting department directors for the effective and efficient operation of areas of responsibility. Managed the research, evaluation, and accountability functions of the school system, including the assessment program, the collection and analysis of system wide data, the definition and implementation of accountability models, the preparation and coordination of statewide/system wide testing materials and surveys, and the continuous collection and dissemination of educational research. Oversight of all internal financial auditing of school and central office accounting practices. In addition, providing oversight of state and federal reporting requirements as well as the development and revision of school board policies and administrative regulations and communications.

  • Accountability Director, Montgomery County Public Schools –

    • overall supervision for six offices that function to lead and direct the entire school system in the areas of applied research, program evaluation, policy and planning, records, testing, and internal audits.

  • Performance Director, Montgomery County Public Schools –

    • direct supervision of 4 comprehensive high schools, 5 middle schools, 25 elementary schools and one alternative school (21,000 students).
